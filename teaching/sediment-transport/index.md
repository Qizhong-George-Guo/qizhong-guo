## Course Overview
This graduate course introduces sediment transport as a fluidâ€“particle interaction process within fluid mechanics. Topics include sediment entrainment, transport, deposition, and their implications for channel stability, scour, sedimentation, water quality, and aquatic systems. 

---

## Course Outline

- Introduction
- Sediment properties
-	Review of Fluid Mechanics
- Particle settling 
-	Scour criteria and design of stable channels
-	Alluvial bed forms and flow resistance
-	Bed load 
-	Suspended load
-	Total load
-	Channel aggregation and degradation (HEC-RAS Program)
-	Overland erosion processes
-	Reservoir sedimentation and management
-	Solids deposition and flushing in sewers
-	Cohesive Sediment 
-	Bridge scour prediction and protection
-	Dam removal and stream restoration

*(Outline may evolve over time.)*
